### Style Definition
Editorial Sophisticated design adapts traditional magazine and print editorial principles to the web. It treats the website like a digital publication — where content (stories, articles, visuals) is the hero. The style prioritizes readability, visual rhythm, and emotional engagement through thoughtful composition, elegant typography, and restrained sophistication. It is commonly used for fashion, culture, lifestyle, art, and high-end brand websites that want to convey quality, intellect, and timeless elegance.

Core spirit: **Content is king, presented with refinement.** Design should elevate the story, guide the eye gracefully, and create a premium, immersive reading experience without overwhelming the user.

### Core Visual Characteristics (Must Strictly Follow)
- **Strong Typographic Hierarchy**: Large, elegant headlines paired with highly readable body text. Contrasting font sizes and weights create clear visual flow.
- **Generous Whitespace**: Ample negative space around text and images to improve readability and give a luxurious, airy feel.
- **Sophisticated Layout**: Multi-column or magazine-style grids, asymmetrical but balanced compositions, and thoughtful rhythm between text and imagery.
- **Color Palette**: Restrained and elegant — often monochromatic, earth tones, deep neutrals, or soft sophisticated accents (black, off-white, warm gray, deep burgundy, navy, or muted gold). Avoid bright or clashing colors.
- **High-Quality Imagery**: Large, atmospheric photography or illustrations that support the content. Images are carefully cropped, often full-bleed or placed in clean frames.
- **Typography**: Mix of elegant serif headlines (for sophistication) with clean, legible sans-serif body text. Excellent kerning, line spacing, and contrast for long-form readability.
- **Subtle Details**: Refined micro-interactions, smooth scrolling, and elegant hover states that enhance the premium feel without distracting from content.

- **Overall Atmosphere**: Sophisticated, calm, luxurious, intellectual, and immersive. The design feels like reading a high-end print magazine — thoughtful, beautiful, and focused on the story.

### Strictly Prohibited (To Keep Authentic Editorial Sophisticated Feel)
- Cluttered layouts, excessive UI elements, or visual noise.
- Bright, playful, or clashing colors (no Y2K, Memphis, or neon).
- Heavy shadows, gloss, or skeuomorphic effects unless very subtle.
- Poor typography hierarchy or hard-to-read text blocks.
- Cheap or low-quality imagery.
- Any element that feels cheap, trendy, or overly commercial without refinement.

### Recommended Modern Implementation (To Simulate Authentic Editorial Sophisticated)
- Use a clean, flexible grid system with generous gutters.
- Combine serif headlines with sans-serif body text for contrast and elegance.
- Prioritize large hero images or full-bleed visuals with overlaid sophisticated typography.
- Maintain excellent readability (line length 40–70 characters, good contrast).
- Subtle animations for smooth scrolling and content reveals.
